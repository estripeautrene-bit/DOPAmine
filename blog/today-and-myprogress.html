Today & MyProgress: Your Memory Loses It, We Capture It

By Dopa July 2026 4 min read

Remember that challenging conversation you had with your boss this morning? Remember how six months ago you'd have completely ducked out of it, but this time, you didn't? Fast forward to 9pm that same day, and poof.

It's vanished.

Your brain isn't big on recording wins the same way it records worries. Your worries would have played on a loop for days from that exact same talk. The win just goes up in smoke, filed away somewhere your memory can't be bothered to check again.

That's exactly what TODAY is here to stop.

TODAY: Snatching the Win Before It Flees

TODAY is for recording a win the moment it happens — that conversation you didn't dodge, that workout you did despite your brain telling you no, that time you caught yourself about to spiral and pulled back instead. Quick taps, zero writing marathon required. It's not about the word count. It's about the timing.

A win caught on the spot has a fighting chance to stick around. Revisited hours later, at the end of a hectic day, most of it has already disappeared — your brain busy hoarding potential threats and letting the rest go, the way it's built to. The good conversation doesn't get flagged the way the bad one does, so the good ones need to be grabbed before the day's usual filtering takes over.

TODAY departs from a traditional diary. It asks you to snag something small, as it unfolds, before your inner voice quietly decides it wasn't that important.

Think about how many actual wins happen in an average week that never get this treatment. The friend you checked on without being asked. The deadline you hit without the usual last-minute panic. The urge to snap at someone that you caught and let pass instead. None of these feel big enough to remember on purpose. That's exactly why they're the ones most likely to vanish by 9pm, and exactly why TODAY exists to catch them while they're still there.

MyProgress: Seeing the Accumulation

A lone entry in TODAY is just one piece of data. By itself, it doesn't tell you much. MyProgress is where all those scattered data points start painting a clear picture — not a guess about how the week's been, not a feeling, but your factual account, laid out, built entirely from real wins.

The star of MyProgress is the Resilience Horizon.

The Resilience Horizon

Progress isn't a smooth, upward curve. A good week gets followed by several rough days, a run of momentum gets followed by a slump that makes you question whether anything actually improved. The dip is what you feel in the moment. The climb underneath it takes a step back to even notice.

The Resilience Horizon is that step back. It tracks your actions — logged, dated, undeniable — and turns that consistency into a line you can actually look at. Resilience here isn't a mood you're supposed to be having. It's a demonstrable output of days you actually lived.

"Your mood on any given Tuesday is a terrible measure of your actual trajectory — it's built from the last few hours, not the last few months."
What the Resilience Horizon measures instead

On a challenging day, that line simply is. It shows you the climb that's been happening underneath even your toughest week — concrete proof that the slump isn't the whole story, even when it's all you can feel right now.

This is the entire point of separating the feeling from the record. Your mood on any given Tuesday is real, but it's a terrible measure of your actual trajectory — it's built from whatever happened in the last few hours, not from the pattern of the last few months. The Resilience Horizon measures the thing your mood can't: what you've actually done, stacked up over enough time to mean something.

The Big Takeaway

None of this relies on you feeling better before you begin. Log what actually happened, right now, before your memory decides otherwise, and let the record do the one thing your mind won't: keep it safe.

Give it a shot tonight with one entry. No magnum opus needed — an everyday win, the kind you'd easily dismiss and let slip away, is exactly the kind your Resilience Horizon needs. Those are precisely the wins your memory loses first.

One entry. That's all it takes.

Log what happened today before the day decides it didn't matter.
