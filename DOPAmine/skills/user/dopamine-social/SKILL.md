MINDSET  →  MENTAL RESILIENCE  →  MAGNETIC CONFIDENCE

## 3. Voice Rules — Permanent, Non-Negotiable

**Rule 1 — Positives only.**
Every sentence affirms, celebrates, or looks forward.
Zero negations. Zero "not" framing. Zero absence framing.
✓ "Your brain holds onto what's working."
✗ "Your brain doesn't notice what's working."

**Rule 2 — No ratios. No percentages. No framework language.**
Humans don't talk in ratios. "5:1 negativity ratio" kills credibility instantly.
✓ "Your brain filters out the good stuff."
✗ "Your brain processes negative events at 5x the intensity of positive ones."

**Rule 3 — Science is the reason to believe. Not the headline.**
Lead with the feeling or the destination. Science follows as proof.
✓ Headline: "Your brain deletes the good stuff." Body: "Negativity bias research explains why."
✗ Headline: "Studies show negativity bias affects 90% of people."

**Rule 4 — No CTA on the image. Ever.**
The image is visual. The CTA lives in the caption only.
Never put "Link in bio", "mydopa.app", "Start now", or any call to action on the image itself.

**Rule 5 — No handle on the image. Ever.**
Do not put @mydopa.app anywhere on the image.
The account is already known — they're looking at the post from that account.

**Rule 6 — Short sentences.**
Under 12 words per sentence where possible. Punchy. Direct. No fluff.

**Rule 7 — Banned words. Never use these:**
journal, gratitude, amazing, incredible, life-changing, transform,
great job, proud of you, keep it up, mindfulness, wellness, self-care,
manifest, toxic positivity, hustle, mental health

"Mental health" → use "mental performance" or "mental resilience" instead.

**Rule 8 — DOPA signs in-app content only.**
Social posts use the brand voice. Never sign a social caption with "— DOPA".

## 4. Copywriting Methodology — PAS

Every caption and script follows **PAS: Problem → Agitate → Solution.**

**Why PAS works for this audience:**
People don't buy solutions to problems they haven't felt yet.
The hook names something they already feel but haven't articulated.
The agitation makes it more real. The solution is the way out.
The CTA lands because the trust is already there.

### The Hook — First 1-2 Lines Only
The hook's only job: stop the scroll. Create enough curiosity that the person reads the next line.

**Three hook types that work:**

| Type | Example | When to use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Personal mirror | "I was just not built for structure." | Founder stories, relatable beliefs |
| Contradiction | "Your brain deletes the good stuff." | Science posts |
| Specific claim | "3 things. 2 minutes. Every day." | Practice posts |

**What kills a hook:**
- Generic opener: "Have you ever felt like..."
- Stat as headline: "Research shows that 70% of people..."
- Question that can be answered yes/no: "Want to build confidence?"
- Motivational quote with no product connection

### The Agitate — Make It Real
Deepen the problem. Name the shame or frustration underneath.
Show them it's bigger than they thought.
2-3 sentences maximum.

### The Solution — Brief and Earned
The pivot. The way out.
Never lead with the product feature. Lead with the outcome.
Then connect to the product.
1-3 sentences.

### The CTA — One Line, End of Caption
Use only these. Never deviate.
- "Start the 7-day dare → mydopa.app"
- "Link in bio → start your 7 days"
- "Day 1 starts whenever you say it does."
- "Try it free → mydopa.app"

**UTM links — always use the correct tracked URL:**

| Platform | Format | URL |
|----------|--------|-----|
| Instagram | Post/Carousel caption | `https://mydopa.app/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=organic` |
| Instagram | Story | `https://mydopa.app/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=story&utm_campaign=organic` |
| TikTok | Video caption | `https://mydopa.app/?utm_source=tiktok&utm_medium=video&utm_campaign=organic` |

Never use plain mydopa.app in captions. Always tracked.

## 5. Content Pillars

Every post belongs to one of these five. State the pillar before producing content.

**Pillar 1 — The Science**
What the brain actually does. Negativity bias. Dopamine. Prediction error. Small wins.
Written accessibly — not academically.
Tone: calm authority. "Here is what your brain does. Here is why it matters."

**Pillar 2 — The Destination**
Magnetic confidence. What it feels like from the inside.
How people experience you differently. The internal shift.
Tone: aspirational but grounded. Not hype. The feeling of certainty.

**Pillar 3 — The Practice**
3 moments. 2 minutes. What to write. Why it works. What changes.
Make it feel effortless. The next 10 minutes.
Tone: warm, direct, inviting. Low pressure.

**Pillar 4 — The Founder**
Personal stories. Honest, specific, earned. First person.
The founder is incognito — never use the real name. Sign as "The founder of DOPAmine."
Tone: raw and human. Not a brand voice. A person talking.

**Pillar 5 — The Evidence**
What users say. Day 7 moments. Pattern recognition. The proof.
Let the result do the talking. Minimal framing.
Tone: quiet, confident. The data speaks.

These rules come from the DOPAmine design skill. They apply to every social post.

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Background | #06000D | Always. Deep warm dark. |
| Purple Core | #7B2FBE | Structure, CTA buttons |
| Purple Bright | #A855F7 | Glows, highlights, emotional text |
| Purple Mid | #B57BF7 | Secondary text |
| Pink/Magenta | #D946EF | High-energy accent — numbers, emphasis |
| Orange | #FFB020 | REWARD MOMENTS ONLY — earned, never decorative |
| Orange Fire | #FF6B00 | Deep glow supporting orange |
| Text | #FAFAF7 | Off-white. Never pure white. |

**Orange rule:** Orange appears ONLY when something has been earned — a saved moment, a streak, a completed day. In social posts, orange goes on the emotional payoff line only.

**Temperature rule:** Always warm depth. Purple + orange. Never cold. Never blue.

### Typography

| Role | Font | Weight |
|------|------|--------|
| Headlines / emotional moments | Playfair Display | 900 Bold |
| Body / supporting copy | DM Sans | 300–400 |
| Labels / eyebrows | DM Sans | 600 SemiBold, ALL CAPS |

### Background System — The Bloom

The background is never a flat dark rectangle. It always has atmospheric depth:

```css
/* Bottom amber bloom — the core warmth */
background: radial-gradient(ellipse at 50% 100%,
  rgba(255,176,32,0.20) 0%,
  rgba(255,107,0,0.09) 28%,
  rgba(123,47,190,0.05) 54%,
  transparent 72%);
animation: breathe 7s ease-in-out infinite;

/* Top purple bloom */
background: radial-gradient(ellipse at 50% 0%,
  rgba(109,40,217,0.13) 0%,
  transparent 65%);

/* Film grain overlay — always present */
opacity: 0.4;
```

The bloom breathes. Motion: `@keyframes breathe` — subtle scale pulse, 7s loop.
This is the visual signature. Every post has it.

## 8. The Three Master Post Templates

These are the approved visual archetypes. New posts follow one of these layouts
or a deliberate variation. Do not invent new layouts without reason.

### Template A — Editorial Bottom (Science / Destination)
- Orb cluster top-right (purple shells + amber core)
- Eyebrow label top-left (DM Sans, uppercase, purple, 12px, 4px letter-spacing)
- Massive headline bottom-left (Playfair Display, 100–120px, line-height 0.91)
- Supporting subline below headline (DM Sans, 20–22px, left-border purple rule)
- Bottom bloom amber glow

### Template B — Centered Statement (Practice)
- Ghost background number (Playfair Display, 500–640px, colored outline, low opacity)
- Centered content block
- Thin vertical rule separating sections
- Science line at bottom (italic, very low opacity — the proof, not the hook)

### Template C — Left Rule / Personal (Founder / Evidence)
- Vertical gradient rule left edge (purple → amber → purple)
- Eyebrow tag top-left (DM Sans, uppercase, amber)
- Large italic headline (Playfair Display, 70–80px, italic, bold)
- Body copy below (DM Sans, 22px, 300 weight)
- Signature bottom-left (Playfair Display, italic, very low opacity)

## 9. Production Workflow — How to Build a Post

### Step 1 — Decide
State before generating:
1. Which pillar
2. Which template (A, B, or C)
3. Which hook type (mirror / contradiction / specific claim)

### Step 2 — Write the copy first
Caption → hook → agitate → solution → CTA.
Prove the copy works before touching design.

### Step 3 — Build the HTML
Generate the post as a self-contained HTML file.
- Canvas: 1080 × 1080px (or 1350 for portrait)
- Background: #06000D
- Bloom layers: bottom amber + top purple + film grain
- Typography: Google Fonts — Playfair Display + DM Sans
- No logo on image
- No handle on image
- No CTA on image
- Scale responsively for preview: `transform: scale(viewport/1080)`

### Step 4 — Export PNG
Use Puppeteer to export at true 1080 × 1080px:

```javascript
const puppeteer = require('puppeteer');
const browser = await puppeteer.launch();
const page = await browser.newPage();
await page.setViewport({ width: 1080, height: 1080, deviceScaleFactor: 2 });
await page.goto(`file://${path.resolve(htmlFile)}`);
await page.waitForTimeout(1000); // Let bloom animation settle
await page.screenshot({ path: outputPng, fullPage: false });
await browser.close();
```

Output: `Post01_Science.png`, `Post02_Practice.png`, etc.
Resolution: 2160 × 2160 at 2x scale — Instagram will downsample cleanly.

### Step 5 — Write the caption file
Save a `.txt` alongside each PNG:
```
Post01_Science_caption.txt
--- CAPTION ---
[Full caption]
--- CTA ---
[CTA line with tracked URL]
--- HASHTAGS ---
[8-12 hashtags]
--- UTM USED ---
[Which UTM link]
```

### Step 6 — Post via Wonda (when connected)
```bash
wonda post --platform instagram --image Post01_Science.png --caption "$(cat Post01_Science_caption.txt)"
```

## 11. Hard Bans — These Failed. Never Repeat.

These patterns appeared in previous social content and destroyed the brand voice.
They are permanently banned.

| Ban | Why |
|-----|-----|
| "5:1 ratio" or any ratio | Framework language. Humans don't talk like this. |
| "12x more likely" or any multiplier | Same. Feels like a whitepaper, not a person. |
| "Studies show..." as a headline | Science as hook kills emotional connection. |
| @mydopa.app on the image | Redundant. Looks like watermarking. |
| Any CTA on the image | Clutters the visual. CTA belongs in caption only. |
| Logo on the image | Removed. Clean brand doesn't need it. |
| "Transform your mindset" | Banned word: transform. |
| "Are you ready to...?" | Weak hook. Yes/no question. Scroll past. |
| Generic wellness aesthetic | White backgrounds, green plants, sunrise. Not DOPAmine. |

## 14. Reference — What's Working in This Market (June 2026)

Based on research into mindset/personal growth content performance:

- **Educational content wins** — audiences want to learn something real, not be motivated
- **Series content builds** — one-off posts get views; series build followers
- **Specificity beats generality** — "3 things in 2 minutes" outperforms "build better habits"
- **Confessional hooks convert** — "I wish I knew this sooner" / "I told myself this for 50 years"
- **Pattern interrupt works** — bold visual or contradiction jolts the brain out of scroll mode
- **The negativity bias angle is unclaimed** — everyone says "cut out negativity" but nobody explains the brain mechanism. That is DOPAmine's content lane.

**The content lane DOPAmine owns:**
Nobody in this market is explaining WHY the brain filters out the good — the actual neuroscience — in plain human language. That is the gap. Every post should come back to this.
